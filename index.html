The Scorching Rental Market Burning Residents in South Brooklyn

Joseph V. Lingad

Oct. 7, 2022

Multi-Family homes like these ones in Kensington, Brooklyn, are not subjugated to rent control and homeowners can charge home units at market price rate or more. Credit: Joseph Lingad.

August of 2022 saw the rental market in Brooklyn reach record highs, creating dire consequences in Brooklynâ€™s Community District 12, which includes Borough Park, Kensington, Sunset Park, and Midwood.

According to the Department of City Planning, about 58% of residents who live in these neighborhoods are rent burdened, which means individuals spend 35% or more of their income on rent. The community district has the second highest number of rent burdens in the city, behind only Community District 5 in the West Bronx.

The average rental price for a one bedroom apartment in Brooklyn is $3,347, an 18% increase compared to a year ago, according to the August report by real estate company Douglas Elliman. In Community District 12, the average rent is $2,047, according to Zumper, a real estate platform.

Here are four factors that are affecting tenants' ability to either pay their rent or find affordable apartment units in South Brooklyn.

Brooklyn Rental Market

Rent in Brooklyn has increased by 44% in the last ten years.The average rent for a one bedroom apartment in August is $3,478. In less popular neighborhoods such as Sunset Park and Kensington, the average rent is $2,217.

Income Growth

From 1985 to 2020, income grew just 35%, while rent prices increased 149% across the country. The per capita income in this community district is $22,924.

Affordable Rental Units

103,200 out of 2,274,000 apartment units throughout the city were available for rent last year. The vacancy rate for units under $1,500 was under 1%.

Inflation & Economy

The U.S. inflation rate is about 8.2% compared to 1.7% two years ago. High inflation increases mortgage rates and deters potential homebuyers, who then are forced to remain in the rental market, which usually pushes the prices higher.
